PRODUCTION RULES (INHERITED FROM SCU)

1. FULL BLEED — image extends to ALL edges, NO margins  
2. 1-2 pixel thin white lines between panels — NOT thick borders  
3. Complete character descriptions in EVERY panel — NO lazy references  
4. ONE DIALOGUE PER SPEAKER PER PANEL  
5. Speech bubbles: black text, white fill, black 2px outline  
6. Caption boxes: dark navy (\#1a1a4e) fill, white text  
7. Thought bubbles: cloud-like edges (Batman internal monologue only)  
8. Aspect ratio: 16:9 (1920×1080px)  
9. Dialogues under 30 words per bubble  
10. Joker's bubbles: jagged, irregular edges

WATERMARK SYSTEM PROMINENT: Dark grey \+ gold outline, 3% width (Covers, End Cards) SUBTLE: White/grey, low opacity, 1.5% width (Story slides)

**STRUCTURAL PHILOSOPHY:** The Prologue is organized around FOUR CRITICAL CHOICES Batman makes during the Joker's game. Each choice demonstrates one pillar of the framework. The audience doesn't know the framework yet — they just experience the story. The framework is named later in the Playbook.

**THE FOUR CHOICES:**

1. **CHOICE \#1: Which crisis matters most?** → Demonstrates CLARITY  
2. **CHOICE \#2: Who do I save — Rachel or Harvey?** → Demonstrates COURAGE  
3. **CHOICE \#3: Do I let Harvey's legacy die or protect it?** → Demonstrates COMMITMENT  
4. **CHOICE \#4: Do I accept being hunted to save the city?** → Demonstrates MOVEMENT
