# PRODUCTION ARCHITECTURE

Each slide = one Gemini image prompt at 16:9 (1920×1080 pixels). Architecture follows SCU production methodology adapted for DC universe visual language.

## KEY STRUCTURAL DIFFERENCES FROM SCU

**Narrator Device:** SCU uses Uatu the Watcher. DI uses THE CHESSBOARD as the persistent through-line. The board transforms: fragmented (Prologue) → organised (Playbook) → reset (Trigger). Caption boxes serve the omniscient narrator function.

**Visual Style:** SCU = Pixar-meets-MCU. DI = Dark Knight noir — high-contrast, dark blues/blacks, Nolan-esque cinematography, photorealistic rendering with comic-panel structure. Still 16:9. Still full bleed. Still 1-2 pixel white panel dividers.

**Co-Branding:** All slides carry "DI × SCU" watermark. DI is the product; SCU is the production studio. The × signals creative fusion.

**Character Descriptions:** Same SCU rule: COMPLETE character descriptions in EVERY panel. No lazy references.

## HANDOVER MECHANICS

**PROLOGUE → PLAYBOOK:** Prologue ends with black page ("The board is already in motion"). Playbook opens with the SAME chessboard, now ordered. Transition: chaos → order.

**PLAYBOOK → TRIGGER:** Playbook ends with post-credits seed ("After the win, something waits"). Trigger opens with the SAME board in victorious position. Then a SECOND board appears. Transition: mastery → complacency → disruption.

## PRODUCTION RULES (INHERITED FROM SCU)

1. FULL BLEED — image extends to ALL edges, NO margins
2. 1-2 pixel thin white lines between panels
3. Complete character descriptions in EVERY panel — NO lazy references
4. ONE DIALOGUE PER SPEAKER PER PANEL
5. Speech bubbles: black text, white fill, black 2px outline
6. Caption boxes: dark navy (#1a1a4e) fill, white text
7. Thought bubbles: cloud-like edges (Batman internal monologue only)
8. Aspect ratio: 16:9 (1920×1080px)
9. Dialogues under 30 words per bubble
10. Joker's bubbles: jagged, irregular edges. Bane's bubbles: heavy, rectangular

# EPISODE 3: THE TRIGGER

### SLIDE 00: COVER

- **Visual:** The same Batcave from Prologue and Playbook. But warm. Golden light. The completed chessboard sits in the victorious position. A tray with tea beside it (Alfred's touch). ### SLIDE 01: THE QUIET — FALSE RESOLUTION

- **Layout:** 2-panel, warm palette
- **Dialogue:** ALFRED: "Gotham is quiet, sir. For now."
- **Caption:** "Victory feels like resolution. But resolution isn't always the end."
- **Visual:** Panel 1: Gotham at dawn. Golden light. Streets being cleaned. Citizens returning. The opposite of the Prologue's chaos. Panel 2: The Batcave. Equipment powered down. Screens dark. All green. Alfred (Michael Caine — elderly British man, grey hair, kind weathered face, dark waistcoat, white shirt, dark tie) enters with a tray. Tea. Normalcy.

---

### SLIDE 02: THE TROPHY BOARD

- **Layout:** Single panel, reflective
- **Dialogue:** BRUCE: "It worked."
- **Visual:** Bruce Wayne (Christian Bale — cowl off, dark hair, strong jaw, tired but satisfied, black tactical undersuit) sits beside the completed chessboard. He touches the Knight piece (Clarity). Then the Queen (Courage). Then the Rook (Commitment). Then the Bishop (Movement). A ritual. A review. Every piece has a story. The framework held.

---

### SLIDE 03: THE COMFORT

- **Layout:** Single panel, golden
- **Caption:** "The most dangerous moment for a decision-maker is right after a win."
- **Visual:** Bruce leans back. Closes his eyes. The golden light warms him. The completed chessboard glows. The reader expects the story to end here. The visual is deliberately comfortable, settled, resolved. Everything says: this is the ending.

---

### SLIDE 04: THE CLICK — THE SECOND BOARD APPEARS

- **Layout:** 2-panel, tone shift
- **Visual:** Panel 1: A sound. Not an alarm. Not an explosion. A soft click. Bruce opens his eyes. Warm golden tone begins to cool — subtle blue-grey creeping in. Panel 2: On the table beside the completed chessboard, a second board. Empty. No pieces arranged. Clean wood. Waiting. Four pieces lie BESIDE the board, not on it — Knight, Queen, Rook, Bishop. Blank. No markings. Unnamed.

---

### SLIDE 05: TWO BOARDS

- **Layout:** Single wide panel, visual contrast
- **Caption:** "This new board has no opponent. No crisis. No external pressure. Just a question that's been waiting."
- **Visual:** Wide shot: Both boards visible. LEFT: completed board — warm tones, glowing, resolved, someone else's game played well. RIGHT: empty board — cool tones, neutral, waiting, unnamed pieces lying beside it. The visual contrast IS the message. Trophy vs. Mirror. Reactive mastery vs. Proactive discipline.

---

### SLIDE 06: ALFRED'S LINE

- **Layout:** 2-panel, emotional pivot
- **Dialogue:** ALFRED: "The chaos boards… someone else set those up for you. This one? You have to set it up yourself."
- **Visual:** Panel 1: Alfred (Michael Caine — elderly British man, grey hair, kind weathered face, dark waistcoat, white shirt, dark tie) notices the second board. His expression shifts from curiosity to recognition. Panel 2: Alfred turns to Bruce. Full eye contact. He delivers the line. This is the PIVOT of the entire three-episode arc.

---

### SLIDE 07: THE REALISATION

- **Layout:** 3-panel flashback rapid sequence
- **Visual:** Bruce doesn't respond. Because Alfred is right. Three rapid flashback panels: Panel 1: The Joker forced Clarity (desaturated callback to Prologue). Panel 2: Bane forced Commitment (desaturated callback). Panel 3: The crisis forced Movement (desaturated callback). Every decision was reactive. He never chose the game itself.

---

### SLIDE 08: THE INTERNAL DECISIONS

- **Layout:** 3-panel, present tense, no costumes
- **Caption:** "The hardest decisions aren't the ones forced on you. They're the ones you have to force on yourself."
- **Visual:** Panel 1: A Wayne Enterprises board meeting Bruce has been avoiding. Panel 2: A conversation with someone (Selina Kyle silhouette) he keeps postponing. Panel 3: An empty chair at a table — the question of whether Batman should exist at all. No villain forcing these. No detonator. No deadline. Important, but they'll wait forever.

---

### SLIDE 09: THE KNIGHT — BLANK

- **Layout:** Single panel, close-up
- **Visual:** Bruce (Christian Bale — cowl off, dark hair, strong jaw, black tactical undersuit) picks up the blank Knight piece. Turns it in his hand. What does Clarity mean when there's no noise to cut through? When the problem is silence, not chaos? The piece is physically identical to the Clarity Knight from the completed board — but unmarked. Unnamed.

---

### SLIDE 10: THE QUEEN AND ROOK — BLANK

- **Layout:** 2-panel
- **Visual:** Panel 1: The blank Queen (Courage shape). Courage without a crisis. Choosing to have the conversation no one is forcing. Making the career change no deadline demands. Panel 2: The blank Rook (Commitment shape). Committing when the only consequence of not committing is… nothing happens. The most insidious form of indecision: the kind that doesn't feel like indecision because nothing is on fire.

---

### SLIDE 11: THE BISHOP — BLANK

- **Layout:** Single panel
- **Caption:** "Reactive decisiveness is a skill. Proactive decisiveness is a discipline. The framework is the same. The trigger is different."
- **Visual:** The blank Bishop (Movement shape). Movement without a burning bridge. How do you create irreversibility when you have to light the match yourself? Bruce (cowl off, dark hair, black tactical undersuit) holds all four blank pieces. Completed board glows behind (trophy). Empty board sits before him (mirror).

### SLIDE 12: THE PLACEMENT

- **Layout:** Single panel, quiet
- **Dialogue:** ALFRED: "Which piece?"
- **Visual:** Bruce sets one blank piece on the empty board. Just one. We don't see which piece. We don't see which square. Just the act of placing it. The beginning of a game no one else is forcing him to play.

### SLIDE 13: I DON'T KNOW YET

- **Layout:** 2-panel, emotional close
- **Dialogue:** BRUCE: "I don't know yet."
- **Visual:** Panel 1: Bruce looks at Alfred. Long pause. The man who defeated Joker and Bane, who moved the chain faster than fear, who burned the bridge and mobilised a city… doesn't know what his own game looks like. Panel 2: Alfred nods. Leaves the tray. Walks away. No judgment. No advice. Bruce sits alone with two boards.

- **Caption:** "The framework doesn't change. The trigger does."

The board reflects back not what he knows, but what he hasn't faced.

### SLIDE 15: WIDE SHOT — TWO BOARDS
