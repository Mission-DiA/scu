## What Works

### Technical Accessibility — Strong

| Concept | Approach | Assessment |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Technical debt | "A loan you take against your future self" | Accessible — everyone understands loans |
| Compound interest | Architecture diagram showing five years of patches | Visual proof — no jargon needed |
| Quick fix vs. rebuild | 3 days vs. 2 weeks — concrete numbers | Clear stakes |

The financial metaphor carries the episode. Non-engineers will understand "debt compounds" without needing to know what an abstraction layer is.

### Marcus Evolution — Visible and Earned

| Signal | Scene | Assessment |
|--------|-------|------------|
| 10% desaturation | Throughout | ✅ Explicitly noted in character block |
| "Not hollow, distant — present, engaged" | Scene 7 | ✅ Direct contrast to S1 |
| Others defer to him | Scene 8 | ✅ Senior architect seeks his time |
| Reena's question | Scene 4 | ✅ Shows his attention is rare/valuable |

His transformation isn't told — it's shown through behavior and others' reactions.

### Coulson vs. Marcus — Distinct Roles

| Mentor | Domain | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Marcus** | Engineering truth | "What you build first determines what you can build later" |
| **Coulson** | Career truth | "What you build into the system, you build into yourself" |

No overlap. Marcus teaches systems. Coulson teaches self. Perfect division.

### Reena's Moment — Lands Naturally

Her question ("How do you get Marcus to notice your code?") reveals Marcus's status without exposition. We learn:
- His attention is rare (two years, no comments)
- His mentorship is earned, not given
- Daisy has earned something valuable

The character serves her purpose efficiently. Good call including her character block for potential future use.
