## What Works

### Scene 8 — The Emotional Peak

This is the strongest scene in S4 so far.

> **VIKTOR:** "I never thought I'd be the one they ask. The one they look to. I still don't feel ready."
>
> **COULSON:** "That feeling never goes away. You just learn to act anyway."

This completes Viktor's Phase 1 arc perfectly. He's not confident because he feels ready — he acts despite uncertainty. That's the real lesson.

### Viktor's Evolution — Earned

| Season | State | Evidence in S4E4 |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| S1E4 | Fearful | Recalled in Scene 7: "I expected to be blamed" |
| S3E4 | Confident | Not referenced — but contrast is visible |
| S4E4 | Mentoring | Leads postmortem, teaches Dev, acts despite feeling unready |

The evolution isn't told. It's shown through action + vulnerability.

### Dev's Arc — Complete in Limited Time

| Scene | Dev's State |
|-------|-------------|
| Scene 2 | Eager, shadowing Daisy |
| Scene 4 | Face falls — knows he's the cause |
| Scene 5 | Expects blame, receives responsibility |
| Scene 6 | Asks why Viktor didn't blame him |
| Scene 7 | Understands culture-building |
| Scene 10 | Documentation complete — contributing |

7 appearances. Full arc. No fat.

### The Replication Chain

Coulson → Viktor → Dev

Each passes the culture forward, adding their own understanding. Scene 9's insight:

> "Culture isn't copied. It's replicated. Each person adds their own understanding."

This is the thesis of the episode. It lands.

### Blame Culture Line — Powerful

Scene 6:
> "Blame builds fear. Fear builds silence. Silence builds failures that no one sees coming."

This is quotable. This is the kind of line readers remember.
